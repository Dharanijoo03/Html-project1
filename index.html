Love birds

Lovebirds are 13 to 17 cm (5 to 7 in) in length,
up to 24 cm in wingspan with 9 cm for a single wing and 40 to 60 g (11⁄2 to 2 oz) in weight.
They are among the smallest parrots, characterised by a stocky build,
a short blunt tail, and a relatively large, sharp beak.
Lovebird is the common name for the genus Agapornis,
a small group of parrots in the Old World parrot family Psittaculidae.
Of the nine species in the genus, all are native to the African continent,
with the grey-headed lovebird being native to the African island of Madagascar.Wikipedia.page

Housing and Environment...

  1. Lovebirds require an appropriately sized cage or aviary
  2. Minimum recommended space per bird is 1 m×1 m×1 m
  3. Lovebird's beaks are made of keratin, which grows continuously
